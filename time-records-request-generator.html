Stage 2 — Time & Billing Records Request

Time-Records & Billing-Records Request Generator

Generate a formal letter to a former bankruptcy attorney or firm requesting time entries, billing narratives, fee-application workpapers, invoice drafts, client ledger records, retainer accounting, and underlying billing-software data. Cites Rule 1.5 (reasonable fees) and Rule 1.16(d) (records on termination); adds an 11 U.S.C. § 329 hook for active bankruptcy matters. Self-contained, no signup, nothing leaves your browser.

Letter Inputs

Sender (You)

If you signed the engagement personally, leave entity blank and use "individual former client." If you signed on behalf of a business entity, use a phrase like "principal of [Entity]" and fill in Entity below.

Recipient (Former Firm)

Recommended: include any attorney who billed time on your matter. The Rule 1.5 reasonable-fee duty runs to each lawyer who billed.

Engagement

If representation is still active or end date is unclear, leave blank — the letter will use "to the present."

If "Yes," the letter adds a paragraph referencing the court's continuing jurisdiction under 11 U.S.C. § 329 to review and order disgorgement of any fee that exceeds the reasonable value of services rendered.

Authority & Deadline

For any state not listed, the default ABA Model Rule text is used. Most states have adopted Rules 1.5 and 1.16(d) verbatim or with minor variations. Verify the rule text for your state at the bar's site before sending.

Default = 14 business days from today. Adjust if your jurisdiction or context requires shorter (e.g., active § 329 review, scheduled fee-dispute resolution hearing).

Records Requested

The default list targets the seven core categories of time and billing records relevant to a Rule 1.5 reasonableness review and any § 329 fee inquiry. Edit, add, or remove as needed for your matter.
