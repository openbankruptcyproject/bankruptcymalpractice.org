Educational template, not legal advice. This tool produces draft text the user copies into the public submission portal at consumerfinance.gov. The federal Consumer Financial Protection Bureau accepts public submissions from consumers about debt-relief providers as a routine consumer-information function. Bankruptcy preparers fall under federal disclosure rules at 11 U.S.C. §§ 526-528. This tool does not provide legal advice; consult licensed counsel for your specific situation.

Before you submit: review every word. The federal portal makes some submission information public on its consumer database (de-identified). Make sure your narrative is factual and that you have not included sensitive personal information you did not intend to disclose.
About the federal portal. The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau accepts public consumer submissions at consumerfinance.gov/complaint. After submission, the agency typically forwards the submission to the subject business and asks for a response. Outcomes vary; the agency does not provide individual legal remedies but does aggregate submissions for regulatory pattern analysis. For specific legal action, retain licensed counsel.
