Letter Inputs

Employee (You)

Employer

If you know the HR director's name, include it on the first line. Otherwise "Attn: Human Resources" is fine.

Employment Period

Leave blank if currently employed.

Jurisdiction

Drives the statutory cite block and approximate response window. For any state not listed, the letter falls back to a generic FLSA recordkeeping reference (29 U.S.C. § 211(c); 29 C.F.R. Part 516).

Records Requested

Used for W-2/1099/payroll scope. Be specific to keep the response manageable.

Format & Purpose

Optional, but a brief purpose statement helps HR scope the response. You are not legally required to disclose your reason in most jurisdictions.

Quick reference

Most U.S. states give employees a statutory right to inspect (and often copy) their own personnel file. Time limits and copy-fee rules vary by state.

  • Wisconsin: 7 working days from request
  • Illinois: 7 working days (extendable to 14)
  • Kansas / Missouri / others: "reasonable time" — usually 14–30 days
  • Send by certified mail, return-receipt requested
  • Keep a copy of the letter and the certified-mail receipt
  • If the employer denies access, contact your state department of labor
