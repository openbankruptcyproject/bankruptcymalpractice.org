About fee arbitration: Voluntary fee arbitration is a separate state-bar track from disciplinary administration. It exists specifically to resolve fee disputes between clients and attorneys, usually faster and cheaper than court litigation.
  • Most state programs are free or low-cost for consumers.
  • Arbitration is typically binding only when both sides agree to be bound; some states allow non-binding awards subject to trial de novo.
  • Filing an arbitration request is not a substitute for a disciplinary complaint. The two paths are separate and can run in parallel.
  • Verify program rules, deadlines, and forms on your state bar's website before filing.
