Why use an exhibit cover sheet?

A cover sheet on top of every attachment — court, bar, BBB, or business — is a small habit that pays back many times over. Use exhibit cover sheets for any attachment to court, bar, or business communications — preserves chain of custody and aids the reader.

  • Chain of custody. A short authentication note (where the document came from, when, how) is the cheapest form of provenance.
  • Reader navigation. Big bold "EXHIBIT A" pages let a reviewer flip through a thick stack and find what you cited without re-reading the body.
  • Bates / page-count discipline. Page counts and a numbering scheme let you and your recipient confirm nothing was lost or substituted.
  • Self-documenting record. Even for personal files, future-you (or your eventual lawyer) will thank present-you for labeling each artifact at the moment of capture.
