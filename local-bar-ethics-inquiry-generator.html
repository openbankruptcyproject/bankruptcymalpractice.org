What is a local bar ethics inquiry, and why is it different from a state-bar complaint?
  • Local bars are voluntary professional associations (membership-based) - state bars are mandatory licensing bodies.
  • Outcomes are NOT formal discipline. Local bar ethics committees cannot suspend, disbar, or impose sanctions. They issue advisory opinions and committee findings.
  • Practitioner-community weight. Local bar attention circulates within the local practice community - referrals, judges, opposing counsel, CLE programs. That is real, even when it is not formal.
  • Many local bars also operate fee-dispute resolution programs - a separate, often parallel surface for fee retention disputes.
  • Use case: a parallel pressure surface alongside state-bar discipline. Different audience, different timeline, different consequences. Tone is inquiry, not accusation.

1. Your Information

The corporate or LLC entity that was the subject attorney's client.

2. Local Bar Routing

Helps identify the appropriate local bar association. The local bar should be in the metro area where the subject attorney practices, not necessarily where you live.

Each local bar runs its ethics committee and (where offered) its fee-dispute program independently. The form auto-fills routing, salutation, and submission information.

3. Subject Attorney

One attorney per inquiry. File separate inquiries for additional attorneys.

Used in rule citations. State-bar discipline is handled by the state's disciplinary authority - this inquiry is to the local bar association, a separate body.

4. Conduct Concerns

Select each category of conduct at issue. The narrative below should tie specific dated events to each selected concern.

5. Brief Narrative

Keep it factual, dated, and concise. Local bar ethics committees screen for whether to engage further - this is the screening document, not the full case.

6. Parallel Complaints (Coordination)

Local bar ethics committees prefer to know about parallel filings. This is coordination, not duplication.

Privacy reminder: Local bar ethics inquiries are handled by committee volunteers under the bar's confidentiality rules. Do not include sensitive personal data (SSN, account numbers, minors' identifying information) that is not material to the conduct concern. Each local bar has its own intake-confidentiality policy; check the submission portal before filing.
