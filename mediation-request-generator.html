Why mediation?

Mediation is voluntary, fast, and private. It does not waive disciplinary or court remedies. Use it when you believe the relationship can yield a settlement without escalation.

Most attorneys take mediation requests seriously because litigation is more expensive, slower, and creates a public record. A mediation proposal demonstrates good faith and is often viewed favorably by both bar grievance committees and courts if the dispute later escalates.

If the other side declines or does not respond, you have lost nothing and gained a documented record of having attempted constructive resolution before pursuing other surfaces.
