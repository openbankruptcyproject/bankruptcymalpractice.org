Status inquiries are NOT bar complaints. Tone matters. Save adversarial framing for after the response (or non-response). Document the request in writing — verbal asks evaporate.
  • Keep the language professional and neutral. This is a routine inquiry, not an accusation.
  • A written record establishes the date and content of your request, which matters if you later need to escalate.
  • If the response is incomplete, evasive, or never arrives, you can move to a formal records request, a fee-dispute letter, or — only if facts warrant it — a disciplinary referral.
  • Sending the same content by email and certified mail creates two independent records of delivery.
