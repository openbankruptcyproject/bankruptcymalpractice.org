HIPAA Right of Access — Quick Facts

HIPAA right-of-access is a federal individual right under 45 C.F.R. § 164.524. Providers must respond within 30 days (extendable once by an additional 30 days, with written notice).

Providers can charge only a reasonable, cost-based fee for copies — not for retrieval, search, or labor. Per-page fees that include labor or chart-pull costs are improper.

If a provider refuses, ignores, or overcharges, you can file a complaint with HHS OCR (Office for Civil Rights) at hhs.gov/ocr/complaints.
