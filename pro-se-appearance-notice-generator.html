Notice to opposing counsel ≠ Notice of Appearance filed with the court. This generator produces a courtesy letter sent privately to opposing counsel so that the lines of communication are clear from day one. It is not the formal Notice of Appearance Pro Se that you file with the court. File the court notice separately per your local rules — that is what creates the docket entry and triggers electronic-service rights. Many courts have a fillable pro se appearance form on their website; check the clerk's office page.
