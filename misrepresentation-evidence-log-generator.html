How this log is used

This log records what was said and what evidence contradicts it. The log itself does not allege misconduct — it documents discrepancies for review by counsel, a bar disciplinary authority, or the litigant's own records.

Memory fades; logs persist. A contemporaneous, dated, source-cited record is worth far more later than a reconstructed narrative. If you maintain this log as events unfold and update it within hours or days of each statement, the resulting record is durable.

Keep the language strictly factual. Quote what was said. Describe what contradicts it. Do not characterize intent. The reviewer will draw their own conclusions.
